Rotary Prayer

Recited at the opening of meetings

Inspire Rotarians, Lord we ask
To live as we profess
To dignify our daily task
And serve in selflessness
For fellowship which here we share
We offer thanks to thee
We pray it will be our care
To spread it bounteously.
Amen

The Rotary Grace

Said before meals at club meetings

O Lord and giver of all good
We thank thee for our daily food
May Rotary friends and Rotary ways
Help us to serve Thee all our days.

Invocations

Three styles for the member giving the opening invocation. The invocation is typically given right after the opening bell, before the meal or official business begins. A different member leads each week.

1
Service-Focused Invocation

"Let us be grateful for the opportunity to be Rotarians and serve our communities. Rotarians dig wells from which we will never drink, vaccinate children we will never meet, and plant trees we will never sit under. May we continue to find true happiness in helping others. Amen."

Adapted from Redondo Beach Rotary Club · Rotary District 5280

2
Four-Way Test Invocation

"Eternal Creator, we gather today as a grateful and dedicated people. Thank you for the gift of Rotary and the fellowship we share. May our hearts be sensitive to those in need, and as we go about our service, guide our thoughts, words, and actions to align with the Four-Way Test. Amen."

3
Short & Reflective Invocation

"Lord of the Universe, help us as Rotarians and humans to do good, to be honest in all our dealings, and to serve others. Keep us humble and grant us the strength to faithfully fulfill our obligations to our community, our nation, and the world. Amen."

The Four-Way Test

Of the things we think, say or do — recited in unison at every meeting

first Is it the TRUTH?
second Is it FAIR to all concerned?
third Will it build GOODWILL and BETTER FRIENDSHIPS?
fourth Will it be BENEFICIAL to all concerned?

Object of Rotary

The Object of Rotary is to encourage and foster the ideal of service as a basis of worthy enterprise and, in particular, to encourage and foster:

First
The development of acquaintance as an opportunity for service;
Second
High ethical standards in business and professions; the recognition of the worthiness of all useful occupations; and the dignifying of each Rotarian's occupation as an opportunity to serve society;
Third
The application of the ideal of service in each Rotarian's personal, business, and community life;
Fourth
The advancement of international understanding, goodwill, and peace through a world fellowship of business and professional persons united in the ideal of service.
